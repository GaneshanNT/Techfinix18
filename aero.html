DESCRIPTION:

I stage:

A glider is an aircraft that is supported in flight by the dynamic reaction of the air against it is lifting surfaces and whose free flight does not depend on an engine. Glider is an autonomous vehicle that glides by controlling their buoyancy and altitude using internal actuators

II stage:

A boomerang is a tool, typically constructed as a flat airfoil that when thrown, is designed to spin about an axis perpendicular to the direction of its flight. A returning boomerang is designed to return to the thrower While the events used have changed over the years, the “modern” boomerang tournament events are:
  • Accuracy
  • Endurance
  • Fast Catch
  • Maximum Time Aloft (MTA)
  • Trick Catch/Doubling

  • There will be prizes given to participants on a random drawing basis.
  • Participants are required to sign in the respective registration sheets at the beginning of the contest in order to be considered for prizes
